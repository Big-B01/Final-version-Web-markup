History of Zandvoort

If you look on the picture on your left you might have already noticed the picture you see here and the picture you saw before are the exact same building the only difference is this picture was taken in 1911 and the picture on the home page was made in 2023. Not much change right? But don’t think for a second Zandvoort is still a old village where the time hasn't passed. Let us show you some amazing facts about Zanvoort

did u know Zandvoort has existed since the early 1100’s?

Crazy to think this little town is even older then the Notre-dame in France, the tower of Pisa in Italy, The Chinese wall and even The White House. Not too bad for a small village in the Netherlands

Did u know back in the 18 and 1900’s Zandvoort was even more known then Amsterdam?

Zandvoort was one of the most well known places cause it was one of the biggest and most popular bathing places. The reason why is that back then swimming and seeing the sea was a luxury that not many people could do due to the travelling

Did u know a lot of famous sporters came out of zandvoort?

  • Elisabeth Koning (1917–1975) sprinter, competed in the women's 100 metres at the 1936 Summer Olympics
  • Bep Ipenburg (born 1936) former artistic gymnast, competed at the 1960 Summer Olympics
  • Bert Jacobs (1941-1999) football manager and played for HFC Haarlem
  • Roy Schuiten (1950–2006) track and road racing cyclist
  • Loes Schutte (born 1953) retired rower, participated in 1976 Summer Olympics
  • Jan Lammers (b.1956), former racing driver (Formula One)
  • Piet Keur (b.1960), former football player
  • Harriet van Ettekoven (born 1961) former international rower, won the bronze medal in the Women's Eights at the 1984 Summer Olympics
  • Danny van Dongen (born 1983) racing driver and entrepreneur
  • Leroy Kaestner (born 1988) welterweight kickboxer
